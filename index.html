Cyrus   Riahi

Software Engineer | San Francisco, CA
The limit does not exist

I found my passion for Computer Science through a deep and possibly abusive relationship with Math. By the time I had transitioned from triple integrals to optimizing trees and implementing shortest-path algorithms, I knew there was no escape for me anymore.

At San Francisco State University I am given the opportunity to interact with developers and engineers in ways I had only dreamed. I am a software contributor to the SFSU Micromouse project, a member of the SFSU branch of ACM, and I worked alongside some of the brightest students in the field to host SF Hacks.

I want projects I put time into to mean something. Maybe not in a larger scheme of things, but at the very least I want it to matter to me. Whether it's software production with a grandiose mission statement, or a web design side-project I can use as an opportunity to learn, what I put my time into matters to me and I have yet to regret putting days or weeks or months into a project.
